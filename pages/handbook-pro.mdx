# Part A: Understanding Mind Measure Pro

## 1. What Mind Measure Pro is, and the value to your organisation

Mind Measure Pro is a workplace wellbeing platform built around a short, daily conversation. Each employee speaks for a minute or two with an AI companion called Jodie, and from that conversation the platform produces a wellbeing score that tracks how they are doing over time. The individual sees their own trend; you, as the organisation, see an aggregated, anonymised picture of how your workforce is feeling.

It is **not** a diagnostic tool, a counselling replacement, or a performance-management system. It is an early-awareness system: a way to notice emerging strain before it becomes a crisis, and to direct the right support to the right people at the right time.

### The platform at a glance

Mind Measure Pro is one product made of several connected parts. As an organisation you get:

- **A continuous wellbeing signal.** Daily check-ins produce a 0–100 Mind Measure Score per employee, rolled up into anonymised team, office, and company views.
- **A live company dashboard.** A real-time read on how the workforce is doing: current score, trend, engagement, where to look first, and what people are talking about.
- **The Content Hub.** A place to publish wellbeing articles and short "nudges" to your people, target them to the right groups, and draw on a shared pool of ready-made content.
- **Conversational Insight (the Insight Hub).** A way to pose a natural-language question that Jodie weaves into matching employees' check-ins, then read the anonymised themes in their answers. A survey replacement that does not feel like a survey.
- **The Alerts system.** A channel for time-sensitive, company-wide messages (office closures, safety notices, supportive welfare messages after difficult news) that appear on every employee's dashboard within seconds.
- **Reporting.** Email a one-page **weekly summary** of the whole workforce to your leadership list, employees can generate their own wellbeing report, and the Insight Hub can produce a cohort insight report.

### The value it delivers

| Outcome | How the platform drives it |
|---|---|
| **Earlier intervention** | A daily signal surfaces strain weeks before it would show up in absence or attrition data. |
| **Targeted support** | Cohort views and targeted content mean you support the team that needs it, without noise for everyone else. |
| **A culture of openness** | A low-friction, non-judgemental daily check-in normalises talking about wellbeing. |
| **Evidence for decisions** | Aggregated trends and conversational themes give your wellbeing strategy a data foundation. |
| **Demonstrable duty of care** | Welfare alerts, support routing, and a visible wellbeing programme show employees and regulators that you take this seriously. |

## 2. What your people do and see

Your employees use a mobile app. You never see their individual conversations or scores; this section is simply so you understand their experience.

### Their first time: the baseline

The first time an employee opens the app, they complete a one-off baseline assessment of around five to ten minutes. Jodie asks a set of validated clinical screening questions alongside more open prompts. This establishes their personal starting point, the reference against which every later check-in is measured.

The baseline includes the **PHQ-2** (a two-question depression screen), the **GAD-2** (a two-question anxiety screen), and a single self-reported mood rating. These are internationally recognised screening instruments.

### Their daily rhythm: the check-in

After the baseline, employees are encouraged to check in daily. Each check-in is a natural one-to-two-minute conversation. Jodie asks how they are, follows up on themes from previous chats, and gently explores what is going well and what is weighing on them. There are no forms or multiple-choice questions; people simply talk.

### What they see afterwards

After each check-in the app shows the employee their own **Mind Measure Score** on a 0–100 scale, with a wellbeing band:

| Range | Band |
|---|---|
| 80–100 | Excellent |
| 60–79 | Good |
| 45–59 | Moderate |
| 0–44 | Concerning |

Alongside the score they see their mood trend, their check-in streak, short summaries of what they have been talking about, and a comparison with their previous check-in. They also receive the **nudges** and **alerts** you send, and they can generate their own downloadable **wellbeing report** covering their recent history.

## 3. The science behind the score

Mind Measure combines three complementary signals from each conversation:

- **Linguistic analysis**: what people say and how they say it, including sentiment, word choice, engagement, and the balance of positive and negative language.
- **Vocal analysis**: how people sound, including pitch variability, pace, pauses, and energy, all of which research links to emotional state.
- **Visual analysis**: how people present, where the camera is on, including facial expression and engagement signals that complement the other two.

A fusion algorithm combines these into the single score, weighting the most reliable signals and coping gracefully when one is missing (for example, when the camera is off).

### Honest limitations

- The score is **not a clinical diagnosis.** It is a wellbeing indicator to be read alongside professional judgement.
- Audio and visual signals are research-informed but not yet independently validated as standalone diagnostic instruments.
- Device quality, connection, noise, and lighting can affect analysis quality.
- The platform needs regular use to build a meaningful picture; isolated check-ins should be read with caution.

## 4. Privacy and the separation principle

Mind Measure Pro is deliberately designed so that an employer cannot identify, monitor, or single out an individual employee from their wellbeing data. This is the principle that makes the platform a benefit rather than surveillance.

- **You never see individual conversations or individual scores.** Everything you see is aggregated.
- **A minimum group size protects anonymity.** Aggregated views and conversational insight only reveal results once a group is large enough (at least five responses) that no individual can be inferred.
- **Sensitive data is minimised and protected.** Audio is processed for analysis and not retained as recordings; data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256); administrative access requires multi-factor authentication.
- **Employees hold rights over their data** under UK GDPR, including access, rectification, erasure, and portability.

> For the full security and compliance detail, see the [platform handbook](/architecture/platform-handbook) and the [security section](/security/privacy).

# Part B: Using Mind Measure Pro, step-by-step

The guides below describe the tasks your team performs. On-screen labels are shown in **bold** so you can follow along in the product. Where a company has been configured with its own profile fields, some filter names will reflect your organisation's terminology rather than the defaults shown here.

## 5. Reading your dashboard

The company dashboard is your day-to-day view of how the workforce is doing. It updates automatically (roughly once a minute) and never shows individual employees.

<img src="/images/handbook/dashboard-overview.png" alt="The company Overview dashboard with the headline wellbeing score, live pulse metrics, the Where to look panel, and the trend and distribution charts" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*The Overview at a glance: the headline score and band, the live pulse, Where to look, and the score, mood, and distribution charts.*

### Getting oriented

1. Sign in and you land on the **Overview**. The left sidebar offers **Overview**, **Cohort Analysis**, **Saved Dashboard**, and **CMS**.
2. At the top, set the time window with **7 days**, **30 days**, or **90 days**. Every metric on the page responds to this.
3. Use **Display** to switch to a large-screen "wall" layout (ideal for a team-room screen) and **Standard** to switch back.
4. **Export** downloads the current view; **Logout** signs you out.

### What each section tells you

Working down the Overview:

- **Wellbeing overview**: the headline. Today's average score and its band, plus the themes currently **Lifting** and **Weighing** on people. If there is a week-on-week change it shows here. This is also where you find **Email weekly summary** (see [Reporting](#11-reporting)).
- **Live pulse**: **Active today**, **Check-ins today**, **Completion rate**, and **Avg mood**, updating as people check in.
- **Where to look**: the most important panel for action. It ranks departments and offices by wellbeing band and flags the **biggest weekly drop** and groups **doing well**, with the change versus the previous seven days.
- **Alerts going out**: any company-wide messages currently live or scheduled (see [the Alerts system](#9-the-alerts-system)).
- **Company overview**: the **7 Day Score & Mood Trend** chart and a **Distribution** donut showing how the workforce splits across Excellent / Good / Moderate / Concerning.
- **Conversational Insight**: a summary of your live questions and what people are saying (see [the Insight Hub](#10-the-conversational-insight-hub)).
- **Voice of the workforce**: **Positive Themes** and **Employee Concerns**, the latter tagged **High** / **Medium** / **Low** by how widespread they are.
- **Office Locations** and **Engagement Metrics**: top offices by score, and participation measures (**Daily Check-ins**, **Median Streak**, **Completion Rate**, **Response Quality**).

### Going deeper

Many cards are clickable. Selecting **Positive Themes** or **Employee Concerns** opens a detail view with a **Generate AI Insights** / **Generate AI Recommendations** option that summarises the pattern and suggests responses. Clicking a department in **Where to look** opens a breakdown by team, seniority, and work mode.

<img src="/images/handbook/where-to-look.png" alt="The Where to look panel ranking departments and offices by wellbeing band, with the biggest weekly drop and a group doing well" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*Where to look ranks departments and offices by band and flags the biggest weekly drop, along with the change versus the previous seven days. Treat it as your weekly action prompt.*

**Reading the score bands:** Excellent (80+), Good (60–79), Moderate (45–59), Concerning (below 45). Treat "Where to look" and the Concerns list as your weekly action prompts, not the headline average.

## 6. The Content Hub

The Content Hub is where you publish wellbeing content to your employees. It opens from the **Content** tab of your company CMS (**Open Content Hub**) and lives at its own address. Its sidebar offers **Home**, **Your Articles**, **Content Pool**, **Nudges**, **Employee Insights**, and **News Sources**.

<img src="/images/handbook/content-hub.png" alt="The Content Hub home page with curated HR and wellbeing news, the latest published articles, and a summary of what employees are saying" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*The Content Hub home: curated wellbeing news, your latest articles, and an at-a-glance read of what employees are talking about to guide what you write next.*

### Writing and publishing an article

1. Go to **Your Articles** and select **New Article** (or **Create Your First Article**).
2. Set the **Audience**: **Whole Company** (default) or **Specific Cohort** (see [targeting](#targeting-content-to-a-cohort)).
3. Optionally turn on **Pin to top** and set the **until** date to keep time-sensitive content at the top of the feed.
4. Fill in **Title**, **Subtitle**, **Author**, and **Publish Date**. Title and author are required to publish.
5. Add a **Cover Image** by URL or with **Attach**.
6. Choose **Categories** and **Tags**.
7. Optionally enable **Share to Content Pool** to make the article available to other organisations.
8. Write the article in the **Content** editor; the right-hand **Preview** shows how it will look.
9. Select **Save Draft** to hold it, or **Publish** to send it live.

<img src="/images/handbook/article-editor.png" alt="The article editor with title, categories, and tags on the left, and a live article preview on the right" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*The article editor: set the audience, fill in the details, choose categories and tags, and write the body, while the right-hand Preview shows exactly how it will appear.*

### Using the Content Pool

The **Content Pool** is a library of articles shared by Mind Measure and other organisations.

1. Open **Content Pool** and use the search box or category pills to browse.
2. Select **Import** on any article; it is copied into your library as a **draft**.
3. Go to **Your Articles**, open the imported draft, personalise it, and publish. A banner reminds you to credit the original author.

## 7. In-app nudges

Nudges are short prompts that point employees toward help and resources. Up to five active nudges show in the app at once.

1. In the Content Hub, open **Nudges** and select **New Nudge**.
2. Set the **Audience** (Whole Company or Specific Cohort).
3. Enter a **Headline** (up to 80 characters) and a **One-line description** (up to 120).
4. Choose a **Category**: **Urgent**, **Social**, or **Educational**. This sets its priority in the feed.
5. Optionally add an **Event Date**, and use **Pin to top** with an **until** date for time-sensitive items.
6. Add the **Link URL** the nudge points to.
7. Select **Create Nudge**.

Your active nudges appear under **Active (n/5)**; you can **Edit**, **Archive**, or delete each one. If you have more than five active, the surplus will not appear in the app until you archive or reprioritise.

<img src="/images/handbook/nudges-manager.png" alt="The Nudges manager with the New Nudge form, cohort targeting, and the list of active nudges" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*The Nudges manager: build a nudge, target it to a cohort, and see your active list (up to five show in the app at once).*

### Targeting content to a cohort

Both articles and nudges share the same **Audience** control. Switch from **Whole Company** to **Specific Cohort** and narrow by **Department**, **Role / Team**, **Seniority**, **Office**, and **Mode**. (**Role / Team** unlocks once you pick a Department.) A live count shows how many employees match as you refine the filters. Content with no targeting stays visible to everyone.

## 8. Cohort Analysis

Cohort Analysis lets you compare how different groups of employees are doing side by side, for example new joiners against long-tenured staff, or one office against another. It opens from **Cohort Analysis** in the left sidebar.

The groups you can build come straight from your own organisation structure and profile fields, so the filters always reflect your terminology. The figures shown are aggregated and anonymised: a comparison only populates from real data once enough people in each group have checked in (the same minimum group size of five used everywhere else). Before that threshold, and in any demonstration, the charts show **illustrative sample data** so you can see how the tool behaves without exposing anyone.

### Building a cohort

1. In the **Build Cohort** panel on the left, open any filter (for example **Department**, **Office**, **Seniority**, **Country**, **City**, **Mode**, or one of your custom profile fields) and tick the values that define the group. Selecting more than one value within a filter widens the group; combining filters narrows it.
2. Select **Add Cohort**. The group appears under **Selected Cohorts** with its own colour.
3. Repeat to add more groups to compare (up to eight). Select any saved cohort to edit it, or use the **×** to remove it.

<img src="/images/handbook/cohort-build.png" alt="The Build Cohort panel with filters for practice group, team, level, work mode, and office, and three cohorts compared on a line chart" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*Build Cohort: the filters come from your own profile fields, so they reflect your terminology. Here three groups are compared over time. (Figures are illustrative until enough people in each group have checked in.)*

### Comparing and reading the chart

- **Metric**: choose what to compare: **Mind Measure Score**, **Mood Score**, **Engagement Rate**, or **Response Count**.
- **Chart Type**: switch between shapes to suit the question. **Line** and **Area** show movement over time and add a **Time Range** control (**7d**, **30d**, **90d**); **Bar** and the others compare groups at a glance.
- Each cohort keeps its colour across every chart, so you can follow a group as you change views.

Read cohort comparisons as a prompt to look closer, not a verdict. A consistent gap between two groups over time is more meaningful than a single day's difference, and a small group's figures will always be the least reliable.

### Saving a comparison

Select **Save to Dashboard** to keep a comparison you return to often. Use **Show / Hide Filters** to collapse the builder and give the chart more room.

> **Good to know:** because every figure is aggregated to groups of at least five, Cohort Analysis can never single out an individual. Very small groups simply will not produce a reliable comparison, which is the intended behaviour rather than a fault.

## 9. The Alerts system

Alerts are time-sensitive, company-wide messages that render as a card on every employee's mobile dashboard within seconds. They are managed in the **Alerts** tab of your company CMS (or via **Send an alert** on the Overview), not in the Content Hub.

> For the full technical reference (severity tiers, audience targeting, data model, API, and the privacy guard-rails), see [Architecture: Institutional Alerts](/architecture/institutional-alerts).

1. Open **Alerts** and select **New alert**.
2. Choose a **Severity**:
   - **Urgent**: safety. Office closed, evacuate, shelter in place.
   - **Welfare** ("We are thinking of you"): a supportive message after distressing news. **Requires support links.**
   - **Notice** ("Heads up"): schedule changes, parking, early closing.
   - **Info** ("FYI"): town halls, event reminders, general information.
3. Enter a **Title** (up to 120 characters) and **Body** (up to 1000). Keep it plain and calm.
4. Set the **Audience**: **Everyone**, or **Specific employees** narrowed by office, division, grade, or other attributes. The screen shows roughly how many people the alert will reach.
5. For a **Welfare** alert, add at least one **Support link** (for example, your Employee Assistance Programme), up to three. The label and a `https://` or `tel:` destination are both required.
6. Check the **mobile preview**, select **Send now**, and confirm at the **Send this alert?** prompt.

**A built-in safeguard:** an alert cannot be sent to fewer than five matching employees. If your filters narrow the audience below that, you will see **"Fewer than 5 employees match"** and **Send now** stays disabled until you broaden it. This prevents an alert being effectively addressed to a single person.

*The New alert dialog: pick a severity, write a plain title and body, set the audience, and check the live mobile preview before sending.*

## 10. The Conversational Insight Hub

Conversational Insight lets you pose a natural-language question that Jodie weaves into matching employees' check-ins, then read the anonymised themes in their answers. It is a paid add-on; once enabled, an **Open Insight Hub** card appears in your CMS.

<img src="/images/handbook/insight-hub.png" alt="The Insight Hub home showing the most active question, response count, sentiment split, an AI written summary, and other live questions" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*The Insight Hub home: your most active question with its sentiment split and an AI-written summary of what people are telling you, plus your other live questions.*

### Authoring a question

1. Open the **Insight Hub** (the **Open Insight Hub** card) and go to the **Questions** area.
2. Select **Write a new question**.
3. In **Question**, phrase it the way you would say it aloud, for example *"How are you finding the new hybrid working policy?"* (up to 300 characters).
4. Optionally add **Internal notes** for your team. These are never shown to employees or sent to Jodie.
5. Choose how often Jodie asks: **When natural** (only if the conversation invites it) or **Every check-in** (use sparingly).
6. Choose how long it runs: **7**, **14**, **30**, or **60 days**, or **Until I archive it**. It auto-archives at the end; responses remain available.
7. Choose **Who should be asked**: **Everyone**, or **Specific employees** to target a cohort. A live count shows how many people match.
8. Select **Save draft** to hold it, or **Publish** to push it live to Jodie.

<img src="/images/handbook/insight-new-question.png" alt="The Write a new question modal with the question field, internal notes, cadence, duration, and audience controls" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*Write a new question: phrase it the way you would say it aloud, then set how often Jodie asks, how long it runs, and who it reaches.*

### Reading the answers

Published questions appear in the Insight Hub. Once at least five people have answered, you will see anonymised, aggregated **themes**, an overall sentiment split, and a sample of paraphrased responses. Below that floor the hub shows **"awaiting more responses"** rather than risk identifying anyone.

**Good to know:** each employee is asked at most two custom questions per check-in, and never the same question twice. This stops cohorts being over-surveyed and keeps the conversation natural.

### The cohort insight report

From the Insight Hub you can run a **cohort insight report**: a structured, shareable summary comparing how different groups responded, with an AI-written narrative and recommended actions, ready to download as a PDF for your leadership team.

## 11. Reporting

Mind Measure Pro produces three reports, each for a different audience. None of them ever reveals an individual's score or conversation, and all of them respect the minimum group size of five.

| Report | Who creates it | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly summary** | You, from the dashboard | The whole workforce over the last seven days |
| **Individual wellbeing report** | The employee, in the app | Their own recent history |
| **Cohort insight report** | You, from the Insight Hub | How two to six groups answered a question |

### Emailing the weekly summary

The weekly summary is a one-page PDF that gives your leadership team an executive read on the previous seven days: the average wellbeing score and which way it moved, how the workforce splits across the bands, engagement, and the themes lifting and weighing on people, with a short written summary and a couple of suggested next steps.

1. On the dashboard **Overview**, find **Email weekly summary** under the *Wellbeing overview* heading.
2. Enter one or more **Recipients**, separated by commas. Leave the box blank to send the summary to your own login email.
3. Select **Send summary**. The PDF is generated and emailed within a few seconds, and you will see a confirmation listing who it went to.

**Good to know:**

- The summary covers your whole organisation. It is not broken down by team or office, by design, to keep it a clear leadership brief.
- It is sent **on demand**, when you choose, rather than on a schedule.
- It contains aggregated, anonymised figures only. If fewer than five employees have checked in during the week, the summary will not send and you will see a message explaining why; this protects individual anonymity.

<img src="/images/handbook/weekly-summary-email.png" alt="The Email weekly summary dialog explaining the one-page PDF, with a recipients field and a Send summary button" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px', margin: '1rem 0' }} />

*Email weekly summary: add one or more recipients (or leave blank to send to yourself) and Send summary. The one-page PDF is generated and emailed within a few seconds.*

### The individual wellbeing report

Employees can generate their own downloadable wellbeing report from the mobile app, covering their recent check-in history with their score trend, themes, and a written summary. This is **theirs**: you do not trigger it and you never see it. It exists so that an employee can keep a personal record or choose to share it with their GP or a counsellor.

### The cohort insight report

When you are running a Conversational Insight question, you can produce a **cohort insight report** from the Insight Hub: a structured, shareable PDF comparing how different groups responded, with an AI-written narrative and recommended actions for your leadership team. See [the Insight Hub](#10-the-conversational-insight-hub).

## 12. Support resources, mental health advocates, and your EAP

Jodie can only signpost the support your organisation actually offers if it is recorded in the CMS. Two areas keep that picture current, and both feed straight into Jodie's knowledge so she can point an employee to the right help in the moment.

> These features are specific to Mind Measure Pro. The university product lists its own campus-based services instead.

### Mental health advocates per office

If your organisation has mental health champions, advocates, or first-aiders based in particular offices, list them so Jodie can reassure an employee that there is a trained person nearby.

1. Open your company CMS and go to **Organisational Structure**.
2. Find the office in the **Offices** section and open the **Mental Health Advocates** block beneath its details.
3. Select **Add Advocate** and fill in the **Name**, and optionally an **Email**, **Phone**, and a short **Coverage** note (for example, "Tax team, floors 3 to 5").
4. Add as many advocates as the office needs, then **Save**.

Advocates sit alongside the existing **Site Lead** for each office, so you can keep both an operational contact and your wellbeing champions in one place.

### Your Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)

The **EAP** tab in your company's **Support** (Emergency Resources) area is where you record the wellbeing programmes your organisation pays for, such as OpenUp or Health Assured. Unlike a plain contact list, each entry carries a short description so Jodie can introduce it warmly and naturally, for example: *"did you know your company gives you free access to OpenUp, which offers confidential wellbeing sessions at no cost to you?"*

1. In your company CMS, open the **Support** area and select the **EAP** tab.
2. Select **Add Programme** and complete:
   - **Provider name** (for example, OpenUp).
   - **Tagline / hook**: a short line that sums up the benefit.
   - **Description**: a sentence or two Jodie can paraphrase, framing what the programme is and that it is provided by the company.
   - **What they offer** and **How to access**: the services included and the practical steps to use them.
   - **Website / portal**, **Cost to employee**, **Languages**, **Coverage**, and any **Phone numbers**.
3. Use the toggle to control whether each programme is **shared with Jodie**, then **Save**.

Keep the cost line honest and reassuring (for example, "Free to all employees"); Jodie is guided never to imply the employee pays. Whenever you update these fields, Jodie's knowledge refreshes so she always signposts the current arrangements.

## 13. Managing your team

Add and manage the colleagues who can access the dashboard and hubs from the **Users** area of your company CMS. Each person is invited by email and assigned a role:

| Role | Access |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Full access plus user management |
| **Manager** | Admin app plus content and insight hubs |
| **Viewer** | Read-only dashboards |
| **Content only** | Content Hub front door |
| **Insight only** | Insight Hub front door |

Choose the least access a person needs to do their job; you can change a role at any time.
