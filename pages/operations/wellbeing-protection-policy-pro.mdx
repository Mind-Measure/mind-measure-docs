# Wellbeing Protection Policy — Pro (Corporate)

Employee safety, escalation protocols, and the case for a no-notification framework.

> **Version:** 1.0 | April 2026  
> **Status:** Draft — internal review  
> **Owner:** Mind Measure Ltd

---

## 1. The Founding Principle

The Pro product is a self-care tool for working adults. Its value depends entirely on honest engagement. An employee who fears that low scores will be reported to their employer will not engage honestly — and may not engage at all. That fear destroys the product.

This policy therefore makes a deliberate and permanent commitment: **Mind Measure Pro will never notify an employer about an individual employee's wellbeing data.**

This is not a gap in the framework. It is the framework.

---

## 2. Why the University Model Does Not Apply

The University product includes an identified safeguarding disclosure pathway — automated notification to a university's named safeguarding officer when compound trigger thresholds are met. This makes sense in the university context for specific reasons:

- Universities operate within a statutory safeguarding landscape (Equality Act 2010, UUK Suicide-Safer Universities Framework) that confers both duties and legitimacy on welfare intervention
- The safeguarding officer is a named, trained professional with defined responsibilities — not a line manager or HR generalist
- Students are often young adults living away from home for the first time, in an environment where the institution has a recognised role in loco parentis
- The Northumbria precedent (OfS-funded analytics project) establishes consent-based welfare data use as accepted practice in the sector

**None of these conditions apply in a corporate employment context.**

An employer does not have a statutory safeguarding role in respect of employee mental health. An HR manager or line manager who receives a welfare notification has no defined protocol for responding to it, and the information immediately creates a power imbalance. The employee has not consented in any meaningful sense — consent given as a condition of employment is not freely given under GDPR. The ICO's guidance on employee health data processing is more restrictive than for other contexts, precisely because of this power dynamic.

Disclosing individual employee wellbeing data to an employer — even with good intentions — would:
- Breach GDPR Article 9 without defensible legal basis
- Create potential grounds for discrimination claims
- Destroy employee trust in the platform
- Cause exactly the chilling effect on honest engagement that would make the platform worthless

## 3. What the Pro Framework Covers Instead

The Pro Wellbeing Protection framework handles escalating concern entirely within the product and through signposting. The employer receives only anonymised, aggregated, population-level data — never individual employee data.

### 3.1 Tiered Response Framework

| Level | Name | Signal | Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Baseline healthy | Score 60–100, no flags | Standard weekly reflection |
| 1 | Early signals | Score 40–59 or mild themes | Supportive weekly reflection, wellbeing resources surfaced |
| 2 | Sustained difficulty | Score 40–59 for 2+ weeks | Welfare weekly reflection, EAP resources prominently surfaced |
| 3 | Acute concern | `risk_level: high` on any check-in, or score < 40 | Welfare reflection held; care-focused message sent; crisis resources surfaced in-app |
| 4 | Compound concern | Two or more compound triggers met | Elevated in-app intervention (see §3.2); Jodie behaviour shift; no employer notification |
| 5 | Crisis | Active crisis language in live check-in | Jodie surfaces crisis resources immediately; employee encouraged to call 999, Samaritans (116 123), or their EAP line |

### 3.2 Level 4 — Compound Concern Response (No Notification)

When compound trigger thresholds are met (see §3.4), the response is entirely within the product:

**In-app:**
- Elevated resource card displayed on the home screen — prominent, not buried
- Direct links to EAP telephone line, Samaritans, and relevant self-help content
- Nudge push notification (if enabled): "We've noticed you've had a tough few weeks. Here's some support."

**Jodie behaviour:**
- More frequent check-in prompting (if the employee has not checked in this week)
- Conversation opens with a warmer, more explicitly supportive tone
- More direct resource signposting within the conversation itself

**Weekly reflection:**
- Welfare template used — focuses on what support is available, not a summary of low scores
- Reflection does not reference trigger criteria — framed as care, not surveillance

**No employer action.** The employer's dashboard continues to show anonymised cohort data only. The individual employee's data is not surfaced, flagged, or otherwise brought to employer attention.

### 3.3 Level 5 — Crisis Response

During a live Jodie check-in where crisis content is detected (`risk_level: high`):

1. Jodie's in-session guidance is the primary intervention — she is instructed to acknowledge the seriousness of what has been shared and provide immediate crisis resources
2. The check-in result screen surfaces crisis resources before the score reveal:
   - **999** — if in immediate danger
   - **Samaritans: 116 123** — free, 24/7, anonymous
   - **Campaign Against Living Miserably (CALM): 0800 58 58 58** — particularly relevant for male employees
   - **Employee's EAP line** — configured per company (see §4.2)
3. A push notification is sent: "We're here for you. If you're struggling, please reach out." — with crisis line links
4. No employer notification. The session is flagged internally for Mind Measure's own audit trail only.

### 3.4 Compound Trigger Thresholds

The same four compound triggers used in the University product apply to Pro, with the same proposed thresholds pending clinical review:

| Trigger | Proposed Threshold | Clinical Review Required |
|---|---|---|
| A — Sustained critical scores | Two consecutive weekly averages both below 25, ≥3 check-ins each | Yes |
| B — Repeated crisis language | `risk_level: high` on 2+ check-ins within 7 days | Yes |
| C — Score collapse | Current week avg ≤ trailing 4-week avg minus 40 points | Yes |
| D — Sustained welfare pattern | 4+ welfare weekly reflections in any rolling 6-week window | Yes |

See [Clinical Threshold Rationale](/operations/clinical-threshold-rationale) for the full clinical advisor briefing document. The same clinical sign-off applies to both products.

## 4. GDPR and Legal Basis

### 4.1 Processing basis

Without identified employer disclosure, the Pro GDPR position is straightforward:

| Processing Activity | Article 6 Basis | Article 9 Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Daily check-in data collection and scoring | Explicit consent (Art 6(1)(a)) | Explicit consent (Art 9(2)(a)) |
| Generating Weekly Reflection content | Legitimate interests (Art 6(1)(f)) | Explicit consent (Art 9(2)(a)) |
| Safety audit trail | Legitimate interests (Art 6(1)(f)) | Substantial public interest (Art 9(2)(g)) / DPA Sch 1 para 18 |
| Anonymised cohort data to employer dashboard | Legitimate interests (Art 6(1)(f)) | Not applicable — not individual health data |

There is no identified disclosure to an employer — so the complex consent/substantial public interest layering required for the University product is not needed here. The employee consents to Mind Measure processing their data; that consent covers the platform's own response logic. No third party receives individual data.

### 4.2 Employer's role

The employer is a data controller only in respect of:
- The anonymised, aggregated cohort data displayed on their dashboard
- EAP contact details they provide to Mind Measure (for in-app display to employees)

They are not a joint controller of individual employee wellbeing data. The DPA addendum between Mind Measure and each employer company should make this explicit.

### 4.3 No consent screen for disclosure

Because there is no identified employer disclosure pathway, the onboarding consent screen required for University (Phase 5 in the University tech spec) is not needed for Pro. Standard privacy policy acceptance at account creation is sufficient.

## 5. What Employers Do Receive

Employers receive a company-level dashboard showing:

- Network wellbeing score (anonymised aggregate)
- Distribution across wellbeing zones (Thriving / Steady / Struggling / Critical) — cohort-level
- Engagement rate (% of employees completing check-ins this week)
- Top themes across the employee population (anonymised, aggregated)
- Trend over time

**Individual employee data is never surfaced to the employer dashboard.** This is a hard architectural constraint, not a configuration option.

---

## 6. EAP Integration

Each company can configure their Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) contact details in the admin CMS:
- EAP telephone number (displayed in-app at Levels 3–5)
- EAP web portal URL
- EAP provider name (for display copy)

If no EAP is configured, national defaults are shown (Samaritans, CALM, NHS talking therapies).

---

## 7. What This Policy Does Not Cover

- **Line manager conversations** — out of scope. Mind Measure does not facilitate or prompt manager-employee welfare conversations using individual data.
- **Occupational health referrals** — out of scope. Mind Measure is not a referral pathway.
- **Return-to-work planning** — out of scope. Mind Measure does not hold absence data.
- **Performance management** — explicitly prohibited. Mind Measure data must not be used in performance review, disciplinary, or capability processes. This should be stated in the employer DPA addendum.
