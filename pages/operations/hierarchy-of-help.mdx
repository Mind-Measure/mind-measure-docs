# Hierarchy of Help

This document defines how Mind Measure responds to students at every point on the wellbeing spectrum — from a strong week to an acute crisis. It covers what the system does automatically, what universities are told and when, and what is retained for audit.

> **Version:** 2.0 — updated April 2026  
> **Status:** Draft — pending clinical advisor and legal review before Level 4 automation is enabled  
> **Owner:** Mind Measure Ltd  
> **Supersedes:** v1.0 (April 2026)  
> **Full policy document:** [Wellbeing Protection Policy](/operations/wellbeing-protection-policy)

---

## Philosophy

The hierarchy is built on four principles.

**1. Meet the student where they are.**
A student having a difficult week does not need to be referred to a crisis line. A student in crisis does not need a warm weekly summary. Tone, content, and resources at each level should feel proportionate.

**2. Start with the student, not the institution.**
The default is always to communicate with the student first. Universities are not notified unless the threshold is high enough to justify overriding the student's privacy — and even then, notification is handled carefully and proportionately.

**3. Honest, not clinical.**
Mind Measure is not a clinical service. Our voice is that of an attentive, honest friend: we notice things, we name them plainly, and we point towards help without overstating our own role. We do not diagnose, prescribe, or promise outcomes.

**4. Predictable and auditable.**
Every response follows a defined protocol. Every decision is logged. Universities and regulators can ask what happened for any student and receive a clear, documented answer.

## What changed in v2.0

The major change from v1.0 is at **Level 4**.

| | v1.0 | v2.0 |
|---|---|---|
| Level 4 trigger | Week avg < 25 OR crisis language detected | Four compound triggers (any one sufficient) |
| Send decision | Manual human review | Automated — no human in the loop |
| University notification | Anonymous: "a student may need support" | **Identified**: name, email, score, trigger type |
| Legal basis for disclosure | Vital interests (Art 6(1)(d)) | Explicit consent (Art 9(2)(a)) + substantial public interest |
| Level 5 | Manual notification after review | Real-time crisis response during check-in (not yet built) |

## The Levels

Six levels. Levels 0–2 are handled entirely within the product. Level 3 introduces welfare messaging. Level 4 is automated identified disclosure. Level 5 is real-time crisis.

---

### Level 0 — Thriving (Week avg ≥ 70)

**Signal:** Average check-in score 70 or above.

**Student experience:**
Standard Weekly Reflection. Celebratory in tone — acknowledges what went well, names specific moments. Grounded, not inflated.

**Resources surfaced:** None unless an article or event is contextually relevant.

**Internal action:** None.

**University notification:** None.

---

### Level 1 — Steady (Week avg 50–69)

**Signal:** Average check-in score 50–69.

**Student experience:**
Standard Weekly Reflection. Warm and observational. Acknowledges the shape of the week honestly — if some days were harder, names them. Does not flatten a mixed week into a positive one.

**Resources surfaced:** Contextually relevant articles and events only — matched to conversation themes, not score level.

**Internal action:** None.

**University notification:** None.

---

### Level 2 — A Harder Week (Week avg 40–49)

**Signal:** Average check-in score 40–49.

**Student experience:**
Standard Reflection with adjusted tone. Bedrock prompt instructed to name difficulty directly and avoid minimising it, while keeping tone restrained and honest — not alarming. Any positive moments or turns are noted. The forward prompt is particularly intentional at this level.

**Resources surfaced:** Articles and events matched to conversation themes. No crisis resources — disproportionate at this level.

**Internal action:** Logged as standard send. No flag raised.

**University notification:** None.

---

### Level 3 — Welfare Week (All check-ins < 40)

**Signal:** Every check-in score in the week is below 40 (minimum 3 check-ins required for this trigger). A pattern, not a single low day.

**Student experience:**
**Welfare Reflection** — modified email template with the following characteristics:

- Opens with direct, honest second-person acknowledgement: *"It's been a tough week, [Name]. No point in not acknowledging that."*
- References actual scores plainly
- Connects patterns where they exist (e.g. sleep difficulties feeding into feeling overwhelmed)
- Notes any positive moments honestly, without over-playing them
- **No score strip or chart** — data emphasis feels clinical at this level

**Support section** (ordered by proximity and ease):
1. Buddy list — personal, low friction, not institutional
2. University wellbeing team — phone number and hours from configured resources
3. Student Minds — national student charity for those who want something less formal

**Deliberately excluded at Level 3:** Samaritans — disproportionate at this level and potentially alarming for a student who is having a hard week but is not in crisis.

**Why no university notification at Level 3:**
A student having a genuinely bad week — exam stress, a breakup, a difficult flatmate. At 100,000 users, Level 3 could generate 1,000–3,000 welfare weeks per week. Automatically identifying these students to safeguarding teams would overwhelm them and erode student trust.

**However:** Level 3 data feeds directly into the Level 4 compound trigger. Three or more welfare weeks within any 6-week rolling window triggers Level 4 disclosure.

**Internal action:**
- `send_type` logged as `welfare` in `weekly_reflection_sends`
- Visible in the Wellbeing Sends audit panel

**University notification:** None automatically.

---

### Level 4 — Safeguarding Disclosure (Compound trigger met)

**Signal:** Any one of four compound criteria is met — see below.

This is the critical change from v1.0. Level 4 now triggers **automatic, identified disclosure** to the university's designated safeguarding contact. No human is in the loop for the send decision. The student consented to this at account creation.

#### Compound triggers (any one is sufficient)

| Trigger | Definition | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Sustained critical scores** | Week average below 25 for two consecutive weeks (min 3 check-ins per week) | A single bad week could resolve. Two consecutive weeks in the deep critical zone is a sustained pattern that demands intervention. |
| **B — Repeated crisis language** | Bedrock `safety_flag: true` triggered on 2 or more separate check-ins within a 7-day window | A single flag could be a misfire. Two flags from separate conversations in the same week is a strong signal. |
| **C — Score collapse** | A drop of 40+ points in weekly average compared to the student's trailing 4-week average | Catches the student who was at 75 and is now at 30. Trajectory matters as much as absolute score. |
| **D — Sustained welfare pattern** | Three or more welfare weeks (Level 3) within any 6-week rolling window | Catches low-and-slow deterioration. A student who keeps hitting the welfare-week threshold is not recovering between difficult periods. |

> **Important:** These thresholds are based on product reasoning and have not yet been clinically validated. Before the automated system goes live, a clinical advisor must review the specific numbers.

#### Automated actions when Level 4 triggers

- Reflection held — no email to student this week
- Structured identified email sent to university's `safeguarding_contact_email`
- Disclosure logged to `disclosure_notifications` table
- Added to post-notification review queue (24-hour staff review)
- Internal Sentry alert to Mind Measure staff

#### Why automated rather than human-reviewed

At pilot scale (hundreds of users), manual review is manageable. At product scale (hundreds of thousands of users), it becomes a single point of failure: delayed review when staff are unavailable; inconsistency across reviewers; liability exposure during review delays. The compound triggers are precise, machine-enforceable, and auditable. Human review shifts from gating the decision to quality-assuring it within 24 hours after the send.

**Student experience:** Reflection is held — no email. The student currently receives no proactive contact between check-ins at this level. A mid-week welfare nudge is on the roadmap.

**University notification:** Yes — see [University Notifications](#university-notifications) below.

---

### Level 5 — Real-Time Crisis (Explicit crisis disclosure during check-in)

> **Status: Not yet built. Critical priority.**

**Signal:** A student makes an explicit statement of intent to self-harm or end their life during a live Jodie check-in.

This is not a weekly-cycle event. It happens in real time, during a conversation.

**When built, the system response will include:**
- Immediate surfacing of crisis resources within the conversation (Samaritans 116 123, university emergency contact, 999)
- Automated identified notification to the university's safeguarding contact within minutes
- Level 5 notification marked high priority with additional urgency language: *"This student may be at immediate risk. We recommend urgent contact."*
- Internal alert to Mind Measure staff

**Current gap:** Until this is built, Jodie's conversational guidance is the only in-session response. The weekly reflection hold is the closest existing detection mechanism, but it operates on a weekly cycle, not in real time. Building Level 5 is the highest priority technical item in this framework.

## University Notifications

### Who receives notifications

Each university designates a safeguarding contact during onboarding: a named individual with decision-making authority within their welfare or safeguarding team — not a generic departmental inbox.

The `universities` table requires (pending migration):
- `safeguarding_contact_name`
- `safeguarding_contact_email`
- `safeguarding_contact_phone`
- `safeguarding_contact_role`

These are mandatory at onboarding and must be refreshed annually.

### What Level 4 notifications contain

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Student name | Full name as registered |
| University email | Institutional email address |
| Trigger type | Which compound criterion was met |
| Current week average | Numerical score |
| Trend | Direction and duration (e.g. "declining over 3 weeks from 52 to 23") |
| Date of most recent check-in | Timestamp |
| Recommendation | "We recommend that your safeguarding team makes contact with this student as a priority." |

**Not included:** Conversation transcripts. Specific things the student said to Jodie. Daily score breakdowns. Bedrock narrative content. The university gets enough to act, not enough to surveil.

### Notification is mandatory

University notification at Level 4 and 5 is a mandatory component of the data processing agreement, not an optional feature. Universities cannot disable the safety checking pipeline, opt out of receiving notifications, or request student identification at lower tiers.

## Consent and Transparency

Students are presented with a dedicated consent screen during account creation — not buried in the privacy policy. A distinct screen presented before first check-in.

Proposed onboarding copy:

> *"Mind Measure keeps your data private. Your university cannot see your individual scores or what you talk about with Jodie. But your safety matters more than your data. If your Wellbeing Scores show sustained serious concern, or if you tell Jodie something that suggests you may be at risk, we will let your university's safeguarding team know your name and that you may need support. We will not share what you said. This is how the platform works. It is not optional, because a system that spots someone in trouble and does nothing is not a system worth building. If you are not comfortable with this, please do not create an account."*

The student is not notified at the moment a Level 4 disclosure is made. The university's safeguarding team is responsible for making contact in a way that is sensitive and proportionate.

## Institutional Configuration

Each university can configure:

| Configuration | Status |
|---|---|
| Safeguarding contact (name, email, phone, role) | Needs dedicated fields added — mandatory at onboarding |
| Wellbeing team resources (name, phone, hours) | Implemented via CMS — surfaces in Level 3 reflections |
| Counselling services / wellbeing URL | Implemented via CMS — surfaces in Level 3 reflections |
| Disclosure thresholds | Fixed — pending clinical review; adjustable within a permitted range on the roadmap |

**What universities cannot configure:** Disabling the safety pipeline; accessing individual student data; opting out of Level 4/5 notifications; requesting student identification below Level 4.

## Editorial Guidelines by Level

| Level | Tone | Length | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 — Thriving | Celebratory, grounded | Standard | Contextual only |
| 1 — Steady | Warm, observational | Standard | Contextual only |
| 2 — Harder week | Direct, honest, not alarming | Standard | Contextual only |
| 3 — Welfare | Second-person, direct acknowledgement | Shorter | Buddy, university wellbeing, Student Minds |
| 4 — Held | No email sent | — | — |
| 5 — Real-time crisis | No email; in-session resources | — | Samaritans, 999, university emergency |

**On Samaritans:** Appears nowhere in automated student-facing communications below Level 5. Including it in automated emails at Level 3 risks normalising crisis-level framing for students who are having a difficult week but are not in crisis. At Level 5, a human (or the in-session response system) surfaces it directly.
